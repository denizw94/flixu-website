### 3. The Fragmented Workflow
**H2:** The Pain of Multi-Region Sites

Scaling a modern, high-converting B2B marketing site from English to 15 different European and Asian markets is an operational nightmare. 

Historically, marketing managers had to export their entire site content into disconnected spreadsheets to satisfy the routing requirements of a legacy TMS (Translation Management System). 

A frontend engineer would then have to copy-paste the returned Spanish text manually back into the CMS or HTML codebase, frantically correcting broken structural tags or overflowing button elements. 

This asynchronous friction prevents rapid international scaling and severely penalizes A/B testing velocity.

Flixu removes the spreadsheet from the localization workflow entirely. Our engine is explicitly designed for headless content infrastructures and automated CI/CD pipelines.

When your Content Manager updates a new Hero H1 on the English branch, the CI/CD pipeline instantly submits the specific component to the [Flixu Context Engine](/method). **H2:** FAQs: Website Localization

- **Q: Does Flixu work with static site generators like Astro?**
  A: Yes. Flixu supports multi-format geometry parsing. You can upload or pipe Markdown/MDX component files directly from your Astro or Next.js repository, and the engine will translate the text while masking and protecting the layout architecture.

  A: Flixu does not alter your repository's linking structure. Because we integrate at the CI/CD or CMS level, your developers maintain absolute control over programmatic routing, domain structure, and `hreflang` tag generation.

  A: Yes. Flixu exposes a robust Developer API and CLI tools designed to ingest structural payloads (like JSON) directly from modern headless CMS platforms, returning localized strings instantly.

  A: The Context Engine natively parses and protects JSON, YAML, Markdown, HTML, and standard string catalogs, making it trivial to localize both frontend codebases and backend data structures.

  A: You define your corporate persona globally inside the Flixu Hub. The Orchestrator mathematically injects these strict parameters—like tone, specific acronyms, and the "Sie vs. du" distinction in German—into every translation payload natively.
