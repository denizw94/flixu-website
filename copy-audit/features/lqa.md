### 3. The Verification Friction
**H2:** The Crisis of Manual QA

In many localization pipelines, QA reviews consume as much time as the translation itself.

When you translate a 10,000-word software manual, a native-speaking reviewer must manually confirm that terminology is correct, UI variables are intact, and tone matches brand guidelines. 

This human-reliant process is incredibly expensive, highly prone to fatigue-induced errors, and undermines the velocity benefits of using AI.

### 4. Algorithmic Precision Scoring
**H2:** Review by Exception

Flixu explicitly transforms the QA paradigm. We deploy a deeply integrated, secondary Language Quality Assurance model that computationally validates the initial translation output.

Instead of reading 1,000 perfect sentences to find 5 errors, your localization managers are presented with an intelligent dashboard. The LQA Engine automatically scores every segment. It isolates and prominently flags low-confidence translations (e.g., < 80/100) alongside specific segments that deviate from your Terminology Glossary, freeing your team to review purely by exception.

**H2:** The 3-Layer Validation Matrix

### Terminology Compliance
The system cross-references the output against your active Glossary, flagging deviations for reviewer attention.

### Structural Integrity
It validates that programmatic syntax, HTML tags, and bracketed `{variables}` are perfectly intact and symmetrical to the source.

### Grammatical Cadence
It reviews the sentence structure against native-speaker norms to identify potential hallucinations or jarring syntax.

By automating the verification layer, Flixu allows enterprise teams to focus human review on the segments that actually need it — reducing overall QA cycle time.

### 5. Frequently Asked Questions
**H2:** FAQs: Language Quality Assurance

- **Q1: Does Flixu's LQA work for all file formats?**
  A1: Yes, the LQA engine operates sequentially after the structural parser. Whether you translate an iOS `.strings` file or a marketing DOCX, the LQA evaluates the localized text against your glossary.
- **Q2: How does the LQA score differ from Translation Memory matching?**
  A2: TM matching identifies how similar a new source phrase is to previously translated content. The LQA score evaluates the semantic quality and terminology compliance of the freshly generated AI output.
- **Q3: Can I configure the LQA threshold for auto-approval?**
  A3: Yes. You can define confidence thresholds in the project settings. Any translated segment scoring above that threshold is immediately locked as approved, completely bypassing manual review.
- **Q4: Does LQA replace human review entirely?**
  A4: No, but it optimizes it. By establishing a "QA-by-exception" structure, LQA highlights only the low-confidence or highly-complex segments that explicitly require human verification.
